iGenics Ingredients

iGenics is a supplement designed to boost vision health, consisting of a blend of 12 unique ingredients, each playing a vital role in supporting eye wellness. Let's delve into the key components that make iGenics a powerful aid in enhancing vision.

Here is a list of major iGenics ingredients used in the formula.

1

Lutein and Zeaxanthin:

Carotenoids found primarily in the macula of the eye play a crucial role in central vision. According to the National Institute of Health, these compounds act as antioxidants and help filter out blue light, which can potentially harm the eyes. This is particularly important when exposed to screens for extended periods or being under direct sunlight to prevent eye strain.
2

Bilberry:

iGenics includes an additional ingredient containing anthocyanosides, which are compounds that improve retinal health and reduce the impact of various eye conditions such as cataracts, glaucoma, and macular degeneration. Additionally, Bilberry exhibits anti-inflammatory properties that assist in reducing retinal inflammation.
3

Copper:

Playing a crucial role in collagen synthesis, this trace mineral is essential for maintaining the integrity of the eye's connective tissues such as the sclera and cornea. Additionally, it promotes the production of melanin, a protective pigment that safeguards the eyes against harmful UV rays and supports retinal health. Moreover, copper has been found to possess antioxidants that assist in combating oxidative stress and minimizing the risk of age-related macular degeneration (AMD).
4

Zinc:

Having a crucial role in maintaining healthy eyes, this mineral is essential. According to the National Institutes of Health, zinc is responsible for generating melanin, a protective dark pigment that shields the eyes from the damaging impact of UV rays. Consequently, zinc supports various eye functions such as the utilization of vitamin A and the overall well-being of retinal structures.
5

Black Pepper Extract:

Incorporating black pepper extract into various combinations of vitamins and nutrients can improve their absorption in the body. This leads to quicker entry of nutrition and vitamins into the bloodstream, resulting in faster effectiveness. Additionally, the extract possesses immune-boosting and antibacterial characteristics, which aid in promoting overall eye health.
6

Saffron:

Extracted from the flower of Crocus sativus, saffron is a spice. According to the Journal of Clinical Pharmacy and Therapeutics, a research study found that individuals with early-stage AMD who received saffron supplements experienced enhancements in their visual abilities. This spice contains various bioactive components that offer antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits, safeguarding the retina and improving eyesight.
7

Ginkgo Biloba:

A study featured in PMC suggests that Ginkgo Biloba may be a valuable remedy for addressing glaucoma. Its capacity to enhance blood flow in eye tissues, trigger anti-inflammatory responses, and offer antioxidant benefits is believed to contribute to its effectiveness. The presence of antioxidants in Ginkgo Biloba is thought to help diminish swelling and support the healing of injured tissues. In addition to these properties, this natural treatment can also be utilized to address conditions like Alzheimer's disease and memory loss.
8

Vitamin E:

Vitamin E, a fat-soluble nutrient, acts as a potent antioxidant in the nasal passages and safeguards eye cells from free radical damage. By combatting free radicals, this compound shields the retina, preventing potential harm and reducing age-related vision impairment. Additionally, Vitamin E plays a role in reducing inflammation associated with various eye conditions.
9

Vitamin C:

An additional component of Vitamin C comprises potent antioxidants that protect the eyes from oxidative harm. Moreover, it promotes the synthesis of collagen, essential for maintaining the eyes' structural integrity. According to the National Institute of Health, their studies confirm that Vitamin C can aid in the prevention of age-related cataracts and macular degeneration.
